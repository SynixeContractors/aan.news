Pirates Disrupt Global Trade: EUAF and CANZUK Launch Joint Naval Mission

By Eugene Blythe

Pirate activity off the north eastern coast of Africa, and particularly around the Strait of Hormuz and Bab-el-Mandeb, continues to disrupt trade between Asia and Europe. In response, the United European Armed Forces (EUAF) have launched Operation Safe Passage, a joint naval mission with the UK, Canada, and Australia, aimed at securing vital maritime routes.

Piracy in these regions has escalated, with attacks on cargo ships rising sharply in the past 18 months. Pirates now use advanced technology, including drones and submersibles, to hijack valuable goods. The new mission, which combines EUAF and partner forces, will deploy naval vessels and surveillance systems to monitor key chokepoints such as the Strait of Hormuz and the Suez Canal.

China has expressed support for maintaining stable trade, although it has declined to offer direct assistance or partnership with any other nations. In a statement, China’s Ministry of Foreign Affairs emphasized backing for the operation but reaffirmed its stance of non-involvement in military action outside its immediate region.

The EU and CANZUK nations, have pledged to continue securing these crucial trade routes. British Prime Minister Olivia Ross commented, “Our nations are committed to working with our European partners to ensure the safety of global trade.”

With shipping disruptions threatening global commerce, Operation Safe Passage is seen as a crucial step in restoring stability, though some are critical that the mission is too much for the only four year old EUAF to handle.
